	\section{REGULAR PENCIL}
Definition: given B,C $\in R^{n\text{x}n}$, the couple (B, C) is a regular pencil if $f(\lambda)$ = det(B-$\lambda$ C) is not identically 0, or equivalently there exists $\lambda$ such that $f(\lambda) \ne 0$. \\
	\fbox{ \parbox{\textwidth}{
		Example:
			\begin{gather*}
				B =	\begin{bmatrix}
					1 & 1 \\
					0 & 0
				\end{bmatrix}
				C =	\begin{bmatrix}
					1 & 0 \\
					0 & 0
				\end{bmatrix}
			\end{gather*}
		\begin{gather*}
			det(B-\lambda C) = det \left( \begin{bmatrix}
				1-\lambda & 1 \\
				0 & 0
			\end{bmatrix} \right) = 0 \Rightarrow \text{The pencil (B, C) is not regular}
		\end{gather*}
